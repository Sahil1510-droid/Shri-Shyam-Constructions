Who We Are

Built on a Promise
of Quality

Bansal Constructions was founded in 1998 by Sh. Ramesh Bansal with a single principle: that every structure we build should be something its owners are proud of for decades to come. Starting with residential projects in Chandigarh, the firm quickly earned a reputation for uncompromising quality and honest dealings.

"We don't just build structures — we build trust, one project at a time."

Today, Bansal Constructions employs over 300 skilled professionals and operates across the entire Tricity region and beyond — delivering residential townships, commercial hubs, industrial complexes, and hospitality properties that stand as landmarks in their communities.

Every project we undertake is backed by rigorous engineering standards, transparent project management, and a client-first philosophy that has earned us a 98% satisfaction rate across 500+ completed projects.

Our Journey

Milestones That
Defined Us

✦
2001
The Foundation
Sh. Shiv Bansal establishes Bansal Constructions in Patiala with a team of 12, completing the firm's first residential project in Patiala.
✦
2005
Commercial Expansion
Entry into the commercial segment with the BDS Mall project — a 40,000 sq.ft. mixed-use complex that became a landmark in Sector 35.
✦
2010
100th Project
Completion of the 100th project milestone. By this year, Bansal Constructions had expanded its workforce to 120 and opened offices in Mohali and Panchkula.
✦
2015
Industrial Division
Launch of the Industrial & Infrastructure division, winning its first large-scale warehouse and manufacturing facility contracts in the Ludhiana region.
✦
2019
Green Building Certification
Bansal Constructions becomes one of the first construction firms in North India to adopt IGBC Green Building guidelines across all new residential projects.
✦
2024
500+ Projects & Beyond
Celebrating 26 years and 500+ completed projects. The firm now operates with a 300-strong team and continues to set the benchmark for quality construction across Punjab, Haryana, and Himachal Pradesh.
